CodersBoard

Website for managing the organization

I’m part of CodersCrew, UX/UI Design team. CodersCrew is a non-profit organization operating in Wrocław. We are constantly developing to create a community where people are becoming prepared to work in IT. There are lots of amazing people, ready to help, answer any questions -ready to create amazing things together. We operate almost as an IT company, with management, PMs, marketers, designers, frontends and backends programmers.

Overview

CodersCrew is getting bigger and bigger. We are communicating through Slack, mails, creating Google Documents, we are working in Jira. But there is a need for a platform to organize better. In the future, the site is to be available for other non-profit organizations to help them with managing. CodersCrew has its own company image and structure. Work on the website began on the part of UX, under the supervision of PM with the cooperation of programmers and with the help and supervision of the client (management board).

Approach

Thanks to the platform- CodersBoard, the management board of the organization will be able to add members, check payments statuses for membership fees, and approve new projects. Members will be able to create their own profile, join projects, check projects progress and build their business history from initiatives in which they are participating in.

Research/ Questionnaire/ Analyse

At the very beginning, CoderCrew members were surveyed using Google Surveys.

Main conclusions from the surveys:

- the section on projects, presentation of projects, description will be important
- users have trouble finding information on meeting dates,
- people do not know the leaders of the departments, they don’t know to whom to report on various matters
- a guide or mentor would be useful to introduce the new member to the association structure

Personas

Based on the research and the surveys, personas were created.
I was responsible for the graphic presentation of the personas.

Event Storming

The event storming took place with the entire project team and the management board of the association. Thanks to this, it was possible to think together what activities we would like to see in the platform first.

Several of the most important user paths were analyzed:
- Inviting people to CodersCrew
- Removing a member from the association
- Submitting and creating a new project

Conclusions:

The most important thing for users and admins is adding new members to association, a view of all tools/programs that are used in the association and a list of actual projects and people who participate in them and their skills.

- Map of adding a new member to CodersCrew

- Map of creating a new project from the perspective of the management

User Flows

User flows have been created to organize and visualize the individual paths of the user on the platform.

Five User Flow were created:

- Flow of creating a new project from the perspective of an ordinary member of the association
- Project creation flow from the management perspective
- Flow of removing a member from the association
- Flow of adding a new member (management)
- Flow of a person who wants to join the association

Information Architecture

Thanks to the User Flows, the vision of individual screens to be designed began to clarify. To prepare for this, an information architecture was created.

Six diagrams were created:

- Members list for admin
- Account settings for a new member
- Members list tab for a new member and a member
- Design tab for the admin
- Projects tab for a member
- Structure of application for CoderCrew

As you can see there is three different paths: for a person during the trial period who wants to be a member, for a member and for an admin That is why the platform needs to have 3 different authorization systems.

Design System

We have decieded to choose ready design system- Ant Design to make coding easier for programmers and designing easier for us. Because our organizations is what we do in our spare time for free, that is why a ready design system is a good option for us.

Ant Design as a Material Desing or Human Interface Design has its own set of rules, ready components and code.

source of the picture: https://ant.design/

Usability test

We tested two people out of our association (potencials members), and two people from the association using sketches (wireframes). The tests were conduted online. I was an observer at one of the test, that was really great and refreshing experience.The form of the usability tests was based on a book: “Don't Make Me Think” by Steve Krug.
